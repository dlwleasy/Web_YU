Yale's 'happiness' course

Her "happiness" course -- which she began teaching live in 2018 -- became Yale's most popular class in over 300 years, according to the university. • In April 2019, Santos had 22,522 new enrollments. But in April 2020, as the pandemic started to take off, the class saw 860,494 new enrollments -- and it only continued to skyrocket. • Santos' class focuses on understanding and letting go of all the superficial notions of happiness, such as the idea that a better job, fancier house, or a new relationship is thenext step closer to happiness. • "All of us want to be happier," Santos said. "The problem is that we have a lot of misconceptions about what really will make us happy. We think we need to change our circumstances in major ways, but often simply behavioral and mindset changes can make a big difference in our sense of well-being." • Some of the professor's "happiness" assignments include deleting social media accounts, daily meditation, keeping a gratitude journal, and investing time in loved ones. • Students also receive a series of homework "rewirements," or practices aimed at helping students develop better habits, according to Santos. These include making more time for exercise and sleep, engaging in more social connection and random acts of kindness, taking time to savor and experience more gratitude, and mindfulness. • The Science of Well-Being, Dr. Santos assigns a set of rewirements alongside the regular coursework (readings, papers, tests, etc.). • Her reasoning, as she describes in the class syllabus, is that "psychological science shows that merely learning about the empirical findings and theories is not enough to achieve real behavior change...The path to becoming happier and more successful requires more than just learning the material—it involves actually putting in work to set up the right habits and mindset." • Her rewirement prompts, therefore, are "activities and exercises aimed at making you happier, healthier, and more resilient" by "rewiring" your automatic habits and strategies.

건강한 습관

디즈니 최고경영자 로버트 아이거의 모닝루틴

  • 매일 오전 4시 15분 기상한다.
  • 5분 동안 웃을 만큼 얼굴을 씻고 간단한 체육운동을 간다.
  • 낮에도 4시 30분에는 일상복보다 몸과 잘 맞는 바지 플러머에 가볍게 음악 40분간 스트레칭과 웨이트 트레이닝을 한다.
  • 운동이 끝나면 자기계발을 겸해 스마트폰을 보지 않는 것이 철칙
  • 운동이 끝난 후 샤워를 하고 커피를 마신다.
  • 그 후 신문을 읽고, 이메일을 확인하고, 인터넷 서핑 등을 한다.
  • 그리고 오전 6시 45분에 사무실에 도착한다.

나의 체력목표를 높이는 4가지 방법

  1. 충분한 수면: 하루 일정의 3분의 1정도 잠을 자기. 수면적자 등의 적당한 운동을 실현하면 뇌와 몸에 좋다.
  2. 활발한 생활: 빠른 리듬을 통해 하루에 5분이라도 몸을 쓰고 지식을 자극하면 뇌기능에도 도움이 된다.
  3. 신체 자극과 환경 자극: 활발한 활동 후에는 충분한 휴식을 주며, 소풍하면서 다른 환경을 경험하면 뇌에 좋은 자극을 준다.
  4. 운동과 공부의 조화: 지식을 쌓고 행동으로 연결하는 습관이 건강한 삶을 이끈다.
